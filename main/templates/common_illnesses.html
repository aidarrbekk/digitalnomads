Common Cold
{{ t('description') }}

A viral infection affecting the upper respiratory tract caused by various viruses including rhinoviruses. It's one of the most common infectious diseases.

{{ t('symptoms') }}
  • Nasal congestion or runny nose
  • Sore throat
  • Cough
  • Sneezing
  • Low-grade fever
  • Fatigue
{{ t('prevention') }}
  • Wash hands frequently
  • Avoid touching face, eyes, and nose
  • Maintain good nutrition and hydration
  • Get sufficient sleep
  • Avoid close contact with infected people
{{ t('treatment') }}

Most colds resolve on their own within 7-10 days. Treatment focuses on symptom relief: rest, fluids, over-the-counter pain relievers, and decongestants. Consult a doctor if symptoms persist beyond 10 days.

Influenza (Flu)
{{ t('description') }}

A contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses. It's more severe than the common cold and can lead to serious complications, especially in vulnerable populations.

{{ t('symptoms') }}
  • High fever (103-104°F)
  • Severe body aches
  • Chills
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Dry cough
  • Headache
  • Sore throat
{{ t('prevention') }}
  • Get annual flu vaccination
  • Practice good respiratory hygiene
  • Stay home when sick
  • Maintain healthy lifestyle
  • Wash hands frequently
{{ t('treatment') }}

Antiviral medications (like Tamiflu) are most effective when started early. Supportive care includes rest, fluids, and fever management. Seek medical attention for high-risk groups.

Allergies
{{ t('description') }}

Allergies occur when the immune system reacts to a substance (allergen) that's typically harmless. Common allergens include pollen, dust, pet dander, and certain foods.

{{ t('symptoms') }}
  • Sneezing and itching
  • Nasal congestion or runny nose
  • Watery, itchy eyes
  • Skin rashes or hives
  • Itching or swelling of lips, throat, or tongue
{{ t('prevention') }}
  • Avoid known allergens
  • Keep living spaces clean
  • Use air filters
  • Take antihistamines as needed
  • Consider allergy testing
Migraine Headaches
{{ t('description') }}

Migraines are intense, often debilitating headaches usually affecting one side of the head. They may be accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound.

{{ t('symptoms') }}
  • Throbbing pain on one side of head
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Sensitivity to light (photophobia)
  • Sensitivity to sound (phonophobia)
  • Visual disturbances (aura)
  • Fatigue
{{ t('prevention') }}
  • Identify and avoid triggers
  • Maintain regular sleep schedule
  • Manage stress effectively
  • Use preventive medications if frequent
  • Rest in dark, quiet environment during migraine
