Abstract
The rapid advancement of digital technologies and Artificial Intelligence (AI) has remark-
ably changed the healthcare realm. Our project’s main objective is to collaborate medical decision-
making with AI. Moreover, it assists doctors in diagnosing diseases and analyzing patients’ data.
The proposed platform combines medical databases, Large Language Models (LLM), and
chat-bots for evaluating information of laboratory and clinical indicators. By operating these
technologies, the system helps to anticipate potential illnesses and recommend applicable treat-
ment plans. The architecture of the project is constructed with emphasis on data integrity, scal-
ability, and integration with the Kazakhstani healthcare system. Moreover, the platform is sug-
gested with a high user-friendly interface for better interaction. The implementation of that
website facilitates to diminish human error, increase the accuracy of medical diagnostic, and
embellish overall clinicians’ outcomes. Additionally, it will contain better personalized medi-
cal care of sick people tailored on its own patient data.
To conclude, that AI based intelligent healthcare platform shows its impact on modern
medicine and highlights its features for data-driven decisions.
Index terms: Artificial Intelligence(AI) in healthcare, Large Language Model (LLM model),
data analytics, intelligent digital healthcare platform , decision-making.

изменило сферу здравоохранения. Главная цель нашего проекта — объединить принятие

и анализе данных пациентов. Предложенная платформа объединяет медицинские базы
данных, большие языковые модели (LLM) и чат-боты для оценки информации о лабораторных

потенциальные заболевания и рекомендовать соответствующие планы лечения. Архитектура
проекта построена с акцентом на целостность данных, масштабируемость и интеграцию
с системой здравоохранения Казахстана. Кроме того, платформа предлагается с высоко
удобным пользовательским интерфейсом для лучшего взаимодействия.
Внедрение этого веб-сайта способствует уменьшению человеческих ошибок, повышению
точности медицинской диагностики и улучшению общих результатов работы врачей. Кроме
того, он будет обеспечивать более персонализированную медицинскую помощь больным,
адаптированную к собственным данным пациентов. В заключение, эта интеллектуальная
платформа здравоохранения на основе ИИ демонстрирует свое влияние на современную
медицину и подчеркивает свои возможности для принятия решений на основе данных.

1 Introduction
1.1 Problem Background
Healthcare systems are producing a large amount of information from different types of elec-
tronic devices and wearable devices. Despite the availability, many health services lack re-
sources for precise decision-making. One of the problems is the complexity and volume of
medical data. Skilled people in healthcare must process enormous amounts of information un-
der pressure. Without Artificial Intelligence(AI) tools, this boosts the likelihood of errors and
cognitive overload. The shortage of AI and developed analytics in routine activities results in
improper patient outcomes. Currently, majority systems cannot provide tailored recommenda-
tions based on individual data. As a result, there is a demand for a digital healthcare platform
that can merge miscellaneous medical information and applies different techniques.
[3]
1.2 Aim of the study and objectives
The aim of the project is create AI based platform that will assist doctors for decision-making
by data analytics. It will include couple of objectives for achieving aim. There are:
1. Inspect existing digital healthcare systems and detect their limitations.
2. Design the architecture of the website and guarantee its interconnectivity and competent
data management.
3. Implement data analytics, AI, and Large Language Model (LLM) model for supporting
decision-making.
4. Develop user-friendly interface for better interaction among users.
5. Based on the results of analysis, give suggestions for improving it.
5
1.3 Significance of the Study
The significance of this study lies in its contribution to improving the quality and efficiency of
healthcare services through the application of intelligent digital technologies. By developing a
data-driven medical decision support platform, this project addresses the growing need for more
accurate, timely, and evidence-based clinical decisions. First, the proposed system can enhance
clinical decision-making by providing healthcare professionals with real-time insights and ana-
lytical support based on patient data. This reduces the likelihood of human error and improves
diagnostic accuracy, especially in complex medical cases. Second, the study contributes to the
integration and effective use of medical data. By combining data from multiple sources into
a unified platform, it helps overcome the problem of data fragmentation and enables a more
comprehensive view of patient health.
Finally, this study has practical and academic value. Practically, it offers a foundation for
future implementation of intelligent healthcare systems in medical institutions. Academically, it
contributes to the fields of digital healthcare, data analytics, and medical informatics, providing
a basis for further research and development.
1.4 Scope and Limitations
This project focuses on the development of an intelligent digital healthcare platform designed
to support medical decision-making through data-driven approaches. The system includes the
following functionalities:
1. Integration of a large language model (LLM) to provide intelligent responses and assist
users in understanding medical information.
2. Implementation of a chatbot system that interacts with users, answers health-related ques-
tions, and provides basic medical guidance.
3. A pharmacy directory feature, allowing users to access information about available phar-
macies.
4. Support for multiple language options, improving accessibility for diverse users.
5. Basic healthcare platform functionalities, including navigation, user interaction, and data
presentation.
6. A recommendation system that provides suggestions based on user input and analysis
results.
Additionally, the platform includes an informational module related to human anatomy, provid-
ing users with visual and educational content. Despite its functionality, the system has several
limitations that require further improvement:
1. The human anatomy module is currently limited in scope. At present, it only displays the
skeletal structure and muscular system of male and female bodies. More detailed anatom-
ical systems and interactive features are needed for a comprehensive learning experience.
2. The AI assistant, although functional, requires further training and optimization. Its per-
formance can be significantly improved by incorporating a larger and more specialized
dataset, particularly focused on healthcare information relevant to the Republic of Kaza-
khstan.

3. The platform currently provides basic-level medical support and should not be considered
a replacement for professional medical consultation.
Overall, while the system demonstrates the potential of intelligent healthcare platforms, fur-
ther development and enhancement are necessary to expand its capabilities, improve accuracy,
and ensure broader applicability in real-world healthcare environments.

2.1 Digital Healthcare Systems
Digital healthcare systems refer to the integration of information and communication technolo-
gies (ICT) into healthcare services to improve accessibility, efficiency, and quality of medical
care. These systems include electronic health records (EHR), telemedicine, mobile health ap-
plications, and hospital information systems.
Globally, digital healthcare has become a key trend due to the increasing volume of medical
data and the need for efficient data management. In the Republic of Kazakhstan, digital trans-
formation of healthcare has been actively developing over the past decades. Initial steps began
in the 1990s with the introduction of basic information systems for statistical data collection,
followed by the creation of disease registries in the early 2000s .
Currently, Kazakhstan is implementing a national e-health system, including electronic med-
ical records, online appointment systems, and mobile healthcare applications. More than 2 mil-
lion users (approximately 17% of the adult population) actively use digital medical applications,
which allow access to personal health data and medical services . Furthermore, over 87% of pub-
lic healthcare services have been digitalized, demonstrating a high level of system automation.
2.2 Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS)
Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS) are computer-based tools designed to assist health-
care professionals in making clinical decisions. These systems analyze medical data and provide
recommendations, alerts, or diagnostic support.
CDSS can be categorized into several types:
1. Knowledge-based systems (rule-based)
2. Machine learning-based systems
3. Predictive analytics systems
The main purpose of CDSS is to reduce medical errors, improve diagnostic accuracy, and
enhance treatment outcomes. In digital healthcare environments, CDSS are often integrated
with electronic health records, enabling real-time data analysis.
In Kazakhstan, the development of CDSS is closely linked with the national digital health-
care strategy, which emphasizes improving the quality and efficiency of medical services through
data-driven approaches . The transition to paperless healthcare and unified data systems creates
a foundation for implementing intelligent decision support tools.
However, many existing systems still lack advanced analytics and real-time decision support,
highlighting the need for more intelligent platforms.
2.3 Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare
Artificial Intelligence (AI) has become a transformative technology in healthcare, enabling au-
tomated data analysis, predictive modeling, and personalized medicine. AI techniques such as
machine learning, deep learning, and natural language processing are widely used in diagnostics,
medical imaging, and clinical decision support.
8
One of the most recent advancements in AI is the development of Large Language Models
(LLMs), which can process and generate human-like text. In healthcare, LLMs can be used for:
1. Patient communication
2. Medical information retrieval
3. Clinical documentation
4. Decision support
In Kazakhstan, the integration of AI into healthcare is still developing but is supported by
national digitalization strategies. The government emphasizes the importance of data process-
ing, storage, and protection, as well as the development of digital infrastructure for healthcare
systems. For instance, edensaulyq is the digital platform.
Despite the potential benefits, challenges remain, including data availability, model accu-
racy, and the need for localization of AI systems using region-specific medical data.
2.4 Chatbots in Healthcare
Healthcare chatbots are AI-powered systems that interact with users through text or voice inter-
faces. They are increasingly used to provide medical information, symptom checking, appoint-
ment scheduling, and patient support.
The advantages of healthcare chatbots include:
1. 24/7 availability
2. Reduced workload for healthcare professionals
3. Immediate responses to patient queries
4. Improved accessibility to medical information
In the context of Kazakhstan, chatbot technologies can significantly enhance access to health-
care services, especially in rural and remote areas where medical resources are limited. The gov-
ernment’s focus on telemedicine and digital services supports the adoption of such technologies.
However, chatbots also have limitations, such as limited medical accuracy and the inability to
replace professional medical consultation.

3.1 System Overview
This study focuses on the development of an intelligent digital healthcare platform designed
to support medical decision-making through data-driven approaches. The system integrates
artificial intelligence technologies, including a large language model (LLM), with user-centered
functionalities such as a chatbot, recommendation system, and healthcare information modules.
The platform is designed to assist users in accessing medical information, receiving basic health
guidance, and navigating healthcare services such as pharmacies. It provides an interactive
environment where users can input queries and receive real-time responses, recommendations,
and relevant healthcare data.
In the context of the Republic of Kazakhstan, where digital healthcare adoption is increas-
ing, such platforms contribute to improving accessibility and efficiency of medical services,
especially in regions with limited healthcare infrastructure.
3.2 System Architecture
The architecture of the proposed system follows a modular and scalable design, consisting of
several interconnected components:
1. User Interface Layer — responsible for interaction between the user and the system, in-
cluding web pages, chatbot interface, and visualization elements.
2. Application Layer — handles business logic, user requests, and system operations.
3. AI Layer — includes the large language model and recommendation engine, which pro-
cess user inputs and generate responses.
4. Data Layer — manages storage and retrieval of healthcare data, including pharmacy in-
formation and system datasets.
The architecture ensures separation of concerns, enabling efficient system development and
future scalability. Data flows from the user interface to the backend, where it is processed and
analyzed before generating a response. This modular design aligns with modern healthcare
system architectures and supports integration with external data sources in future developments.
3.3 Data Sources and Processing
The system utilizes multiple types of data to support its functionality:
1. User input data — queries entered by users through the chatbot interface.
2. Healthcare-related datasets — including information about pharmacies and basic medical
knowledge.
3. Pre-trained language model data — used by the LLM for generating responses.
Data processing involves:
10
1. Input preprocessing (text cleaning and formatting)
2. Query interpretation
3. Response generation using AI models
4. Output formatting for user display
In Kazakhstan, healthcare data is increasingly digitized through national e-health systems.
However, access to structured and publicly available datasets remains limited. Therefore, the
system primarily relies on general medical knowledge and locally available data sources. Future
improvements may include integration with national healthcare databases to enhance accuracy
and localization.
3.4 LLM Integration
A key component of the system is the integration of a large language model (LLM), which
enables natural language interaction and intelligent response generation.
The LLM processes user queries and generates context-aware responses based on:
1. Pre-trained knowledge
2. Prompt design and system instructions
3. Contextual understanding of user input
Although the LLM performs effectively, its accuracy depends on the quality and relevance of
training data. In the context of Kazakhstan, further improvement requires incorporating region-
specific healthcare information, including local medical guidelines and language adaptation.
3.5 Chatbot Design
The chatbot serves as the primary interaction mechanism between users and the system. It is
designed to simulate human-like conversation and provide intuitive access to healthcare infor-
mation. The chatbot workflow consists of that principles:
1. User submits a query
2. System processes the input
3. LLM generates a response
4. System returns the response to the user
The chatbot supports several functions. For instance, general health inquiries, navigation as-
sistance within the platform, and basic recommendations. The design emphasizes usability,
responsiveness, and clarity of communication. It allows users with different levels of digital
literacy to interact with the system effectively.
11
3.6 Recommendation System
The recommendation system provides users with suggestions based on their input and system
analysis. It enhances the functionality of the platform by offering personalized or context-based
guidance. Recommendations may include: general health advice, suggested actions based on
symptoms, and relevant healthcare information.
The system uses rule-based logic combined with AI-generated insights. While the current
implementation provides basic recommendations, future enhancements may include advanced
machine learning models for predictive analytics.

5 Conclusion and Future Work
5.1 Conclusion
This study focused on the development of an intelligent digital healthcare platform designed
to support medical decision-making through data-driven approaches. The primary objective
was to integrate modern technologies, including artificial intelligence and data analytics, into a
user-friendly system that improves access to healthcare information and assists users in making
informed decisions.
The developed platform successfully combines several key components, including a large
language model (LLM), a chatbot interface, a recommendation system, and a pharmacy infor-
mation module. These features enable users to interact with the system in natural language,
receive relevant medical information, and access healthcare-related services efficiently.
The implementation of the chatbot demonstrates the effectiveness of conversational inter-
faces in healthcare applications. Users can ask questions and receive real-time responses, which
enhances accessibility and reduces the need for direct interaction with healthcare professionals
for basic inquiries. Additionally, the recommendation system provides context-based sugges-
tions, contributing to a more personalized user experience.
The platform also includes support for multiple languages, which increases its usability in
diverse populations. The integration of pharmacy data further enhances the practical value of
the system by helping users locate relevant healthcare services.
5.2 Future Work
Despite the successful implementation of the core functionalities, several areas for future im-
provement and development have been identified.
First, the accuracy and reliability of the AI assistant can be enhanced by further training the
large language model using more specialized and localized datasets. In particular, incorporating
healthcare data relevant to the Republic of Kazakhstan, including national medical guidelines
and terminology, would significantly improve the quality and relevance of responses.
Second, the human anatomy module requires further development. Currently, it provides
limited visualization of the skeletal and muscular systems for male and female bodies. Future
work should expand this module to include additional anatomical systems, such as the nervous,
cardiovascular, and digestive systems, as well as interactive and educational features.
Third, the recommendation system can be improved by implementing more advanced ma-
chine learning techniques. This would allow for more accurate predictions, personalized rec-
ommendations, and risk assessment based on user input and historical data.
Additionally, future versions of the platform may include integration with real healthcare
systems, such as hospitals, electronic health records, and national healthcare databases. This
would enable real-time data access and significantly enhance the practical applicability of the
system. Another important direction is improving data security and compliance with healthcare
regulations. As the system evolves to handle more sensitive data, it will be essential to ensure
full compliance with legal and ethical standards.
Finally, the user interface can be further refined to improve usability and accessibility, par-
ticularly for users with limited technical skills.
